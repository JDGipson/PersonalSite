Who am I?

  • Honor
  • Courage
  • Commitment

These core values have stuck with me throughout my life after being in the marines. Honor tells us to uphold our core values and morales. Be honest and always do your best. Courage is to always push forward even in the face of adversity. Commitment is the most important value here. It could mean staying on task when everyone tells you to take a break, or staying with your company and working with them through the hard times.

I served in the USMC from 2008 to 2012. It was a hard life and even harder decision, but the best decision I ever made. I have bounced around from job to job since getting out of the Marines and never felt like I was in the right position. So I turned to software engineering after talking to a few friends that work in the same field. I am enjoying learning coding due to the complexity of it. Complex puzzles that make me think and learn are what I thrive on.

Why Software Engineering?

I am learning software engineering to better my life and create a better life for my kids as well. After working for multiple companies in many different positions I am ready to have a stable foundation that will carry me through life. I don't want to be working for someone for a year and then finding a new position somewhere else due to not being challenged or getting bored with my day to day. Software engineering has opend my eyes to a whole new spectrum of knowledge and capabilities. I already have lots of ideas and fun things I would like to try.

What am I looking for in an employer?

Im looking for a position that I can thrive in with possibilities of advancement. I need an employer that can recognize my capabilities and give me challenging projects to help me better myself in the software engineering field. I will always strive to learn and grow, so I need my employer to strive for the same. Coming in to this field now gives me an advantage due to having so much to learn. "Knowledge is power". It has been stated by greater people than I but it speaks to me on many levels. The more I know, the better I can become.
